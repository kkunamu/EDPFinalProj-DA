National Agriculture and Fisheries Modernization and Industrialization Plan 2021–2030

NAFMIP 2021-2030, as a whole-of-nation plan, serves as a Directional Plan to steer sector-wide growth over the next decade. It will guide the trajectory of more detailed and operations-oriented agri-fishery development plans such as the commodity system roadmaps, Provincial Commodity Investment Plans (PCIPs), and Comprehensive Land Use Plans (CLUP). Thus, NAFMIP is more policy- and strategy-oriented, but not as detailed as previous AFMP iterations.

Further, NAFMIP shall aim to inspire the full range of private and public stakeholders to take coordinated, cohesive, and determined actions toward achieving a common vision and objectives, and to galvanize sector-wide public and private investments and resources to support the shared vision, objectives, and concerted action.

NAFMIP will seek to more than double smallholder farmers’ and fishers’ incomes to be able to meet their family household needs during the period of Plan implementation, through the promotion of transformative interventions to significantly diversify sources of income and employment—and ultimately sustainably lift farmers and fisherfolk out of poverty. This objective will be achieved with minimal impact on the environment and without sacrificing the nutrition and health of consumers. One key strategy is to diversify farming, non-farming, and value adding activities and link farmers and fisherfolk to large private investments such as modern food terminals and market hubs serving as potent engines of inclusive growth.

Adaptation and Mitigation Initiative in Agriculture

The Adaptation and Mitigation Initiative in Agriculture (AMIA) is the flagship program of the Department of Agriculture (DA), dedicated to addressing the dual challenges of climate change: adaptation and mitigation. With a vision of achieving a climate-resilient Philippine agriculture, AMIA focuses on empowering vulnerable farmers and fisherfolk to combat the adverse effects of climate change while promoting sustainable agricultural practices.

The program is committed to advancing climate-resilient agriculture (CRA) practices by providing tailored, integrated, and responsive support services that enhance adaptive capacities in the face of environmental disruptions. These services include promoting innovative farming techniques, strengthening disaster risk reduction strategies, and introducing climate-smart technologies that can withstand extreme weather conditions, such as typhoons and droughts.

Climate change poses significant threats to food security in the Philippines, with observed increases in temperature, sea level rise, and more frequent and intense typhoons adversely impacting the agricultural sector. The AMIA Program seeks to mitigate these impacts through targeted interventions, community empowerment, and partnerships with various stakeholders. The program also emphasizes capacity-building, ensuring that local communities are equipped with the knowledge and tools to adapt and thrive despite climate challenges.

Through AMIA, the DA aims not only to safeguard food security and promote economic growth but also to foster rural development, ensuring that agriculture remains a sustainable and viable livelihood for future generations. By working together with local governments, farmers’ associations, and other partners, AMIA strives to build a resilient agricultural sector that can withstand the challenges of climate change while contributing to the nation’s long-term development goals. Philippine Rural Development Project

The Philippine Rural Development Project (PRDP) is a six-year initiative aimed at establishing a government platform to modernize, climate-proof, and market-orient the agri-fishery sector. This project, a collaboration between the Department of Agriculture (DA), Local Government Units (LGUs), and the private sector, seeks to provide essential infrastructure, technology, and information to improve rural productivity, raise incomes, and enhance competitiveness in the countryside.


PRDP focuses on creating sustainable, market-driven agricultural practices that align with climate-smart strategies to ensure long-term growth. By partnering with various stakeholders, the project aims to improve the agri-fishery value chain through investments in critical infrastructure, farm-to-market roads, post-harvest facilities, and climate-resilient technologies.


Within the six-year intervention period, PRDP is expected to achieve the following outcomes:


  • A 5% increase in annual real farm incomes for household beneficiaries
  • A 30% increase in income for targeted beneficiaries of enterprise development
  • A 7% increase in the value of annual marketed output
  • A 20% increase in the number of farmers and fishers with improved access to DA services

Through PRDP, the DA envisions not only increasing the incomes of rural households but also fostering inclusive economic growth, improving food security, and contributing to sustainable rural development. This initiative is key to empowering local farmers and fishers, boosting rural industries, and improving the overall competitiveness of the Philippine agri-fishery sector.

Special Area for Agricultural Development

The Special Area for Agricultural Development (SAAD) Program is a locally-funded initiative by the Department of Agriculture (DA) designed to alleviate poverty among marginalized sectors of agriculture and fisheries. Focused on thirty (30) priority provinces identified by the Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA), as well as areas covered by Executive Order No. 70, series of 2018, the program targets vulnerable groups, including farmers, fisherfolk, Indigenous Peoples (IPs), and beneficiaries of the Department of Social Welfare and Development’s (DSWD) 4Ps program.

SAAD’s strategy centers on increasing food production, enhancing the resilience of rural communities, and establishing sustainable community enterprises. By providing essential support such as appropriate technology, financing, marketing assistance, and capacity-building services, the program empowers local communities to enhance agricultural practices, diversify sources of income, and improve overall livelihood opportunities. Through these services, SAAD aims to help communities gain greater economic independence and build resilience to the impacts of climate change and other environmental challenges.

The program targets sectors that have long been marginalized, helping them transition from subsistence farming to more productive, market-oriented livelihoods. By fostering community-driven projects and enterprises, SAAD contributes to the creation of local value chains in agriculture and fisheries, ensuring that products are not only produced but also marketed effectively. This comprehensive approach strengthens the agri-fishery sector, uplifts vulnerable groups, and encourages sustainable rural development.

For more information, visit the SAAD Website.

The National Organic Agriculture Program – National Program Coordinating Office (NOAP-NPCO)

The enactment of Republic Act No. 10068, or the Organic Agriculture Act of 2010, later amended by R.A. No. 11511, institutionalized organic agriculture (OA) in the Philippines and established the National Organic Agriculture Program (NOAP) and its National Program Coordinating Office (NPCO) in 2021.


The NOAP is tasked with promoting and implementing OA to achieve profitable, self-sufficient, and sustainable agriculture. The National Organic Agriculture Board (NOAB), attached to the Department of Agriculture (DA), provides direction and policy guidance for the program.


DIVISIONS

  • Advocacy and Policy Division (APD): Leads information and advocacy campaigns, serving as the technical and administrative secretariat of the NOAB.
  • Project Development Support Division (PDSD): Handles project development, monitoring and evaluation, and planning and budgeting for the program.

CORE SERVICES

  • Organic Agriculture Livelihood Project (OALP)
  • Participatory Guarantee System (PGS)
  • Capacity Development (OA Level-Up)
  • Research for Development and Extension
  • Youth Internship Program on Organic Agriculture (YIPOA)
  • Establishment of Organic Seed Centers
  • Marketing Assistance (OA Digital Marketplace)
  • Social Credit
  • Policy Development
  • Advocacy and Promotion (IEC Materials, Information Caravan, Congresses, Summits)

Kabuhayan at Kaunlaran ng Kababayang Katutubo

The Kabuhayan at Kaunlaran ng Kababayang Katutubo (4K) is a three-year special project by the Department of Agriculture (DA) aimed at enhancing agricultural and fisheries-related livelihood opportunities for Indigenous Cultural

Communities/Indigenous Peoples (ICCs/IPs) across the country. This initiative responds to the President’s directive to provide targeted support services, including investments in production and post-harvest machinery, equipment, and other necessary services based on community needs assessments.

The 4K Project is made up of four key components:

  1. Social Preparation: Conducts social analysis and community engagement to ensure effective project implementation through proper planning and selection.
  2. Production and Livelihood: Provides the necessary tools, assets, and resources to help ICCs/IPs support themselves and improve their livelihoods through agricultural and fisheries activities.
  3. Marketing Assistance and Enterprise Development: Empowers ICCs/IPs as entrepreneurs, helping them create competitive agribusinesses, gain market access, and improve income.
  4. Project Management: Establishes and maintains project standards, guidelines, and management practices to ensure successful implementation and sustainability of the program.


The 4K Program aims to empower ICCs/IPs by building self-sufficient, resilient communities through livelihood development, entrepreneurship, and sustainable agricultural practices.

For more information, visit the 4Ks Website.

DA – Disaster Risk Reduction and Management (DRRM)

The Philippines, located in the Pacific typhoon belt, experiences around 20 tropical cyclones annually, severely impacting the agriculture and fisheries sector. In response to the country's vulnerability, Republic Act No. 10121 or the Philippine Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Act of 2010, was enacted to strengthen disaster resilience through four key pillars: prevention and mitigation, preparedness, response, and rehabilitation and recovery.

To address these challenges in the agriculture sector, the Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Section (DRRMS) was established under the Department of Agriculture (DA) to help protect livelihoods and ensure food security. Its core strategies include:


  1. Establishing a DRRM policy framework
  2. Coordinating DRRM activities across all four thematic pillars
  3. Developing a GIS-based DRRM information system
  4. Conducting damage and needs assessments (RDANA, DALA, PDNA)
  5. Issuing early warning information
  6. Integrating DRRM into DA programs through planning tools


To implement these strategies, the DRRMS formed three support units: the Disaster and Climate Information Office (DCIO), the Disaster Risk Reduction Office (DRRO), and the Rehabilitation and Recovery Support Office (RRSO).

For more information, visit the DA-DRRM webpage.

National Convergence Initiative for Sustainable Rural Development (NCI-SRD)

The National Convergence Initiative for Sustainable Rural Development (NCI-SRD) is a unified strategy by four key government agencies—DA, DAR, DENR, and DILG—through Joint Administrative Order No. 01, series of 2020. This revised policy framework strengthens the collaboration of these agencies to deliver more efficient, coordinated, and sustainable rural development efforts.

The NCI-SRD was developed in response to the long-standing fragmentation of services across the rural development landscape. By harmonizing policies, programs, and resources, the initiative fosters improved governance, effective land use, and ecological balance through a watershed and major river basin-based planning approach. This ensures that natural ecosystems and human communities are developed cohesively and sustainably.

The initiative is brought to life at the local level through Convergence Area Development Plans (CADPs). These plans are tailored to the unique geographic, environmental, and socio-economic conditions of each area and guide the rollout of multi-sectoral interventions. Priority beneficiaries include smallholder farmers, both agrarian reform beneficiaries (ARBs) and non-ARBs, upland dwellers, fisherfolk, and Indigenous Peoples (IPs)—key communities that play critical roles in the country’s food production and ecosystem preservation.

All activities are guided by the ridge-to-reef approach, an integrated natural resource management framework that ensures interventions across landscapes—from upland watersheds down to coastal and marine ecosystems—are cohesive and mutually reinforcing.

Through shared leadership, resource complementation, and strategic alignment, the NCI-SRD envisions empowered rural communities thriving in a well-managed, sustainable, and resilient rural environment.
